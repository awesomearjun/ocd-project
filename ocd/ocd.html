OCD is a mental disorder that stands for Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder. People with OCD have time consuming compulsions, and/or obsessions.
An obsession is a recurring thought, urge, or mental image. They can be intrusive, unwanted, and make them feel anxious; such as:
» Fear of things being dirty
» Fear of losing something
» Fear of going out of control with their behaviour
» Agressive thoughts against themselves
» Obsessively thinking that things have to be perfect

Compulsions are recurring behaviours, most of the times in response to an obsession, such as:
» Overly cleaning or handwashing
» Arranging items in a specific way
» Repeatitively checking on things, like if a door is locked, or an appliance is on

Not all repeated thoghts are obsessions, and not all repeated actions are compulsions. However, people with OCD generally:
» Can’t control their obsessions or compulsions
» Spend around/over 1 hour every day on their habits
» Don’t feel pleasure from completing their obsessions or compulsions but get temporary relief from their anxiety
» Experience big problems due to these habits

Some people with OCD also have Tourette's, which is kind of like a compulsion. OCD is a long lasting disorder, but there is treatment.
