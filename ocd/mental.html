Mental illnesses are health conditions, but they involve emotions, thinking, and/or behaviours. They are linked with distress,
and problems in social environments. Half of all mental illnesses begin by 14, but OCD can begin at any age; OCD usually starts at 14 too though.

Just like most mental illnesses, 50-60% of people report getting OCD post-traumatic events, such as:
» Illness or hospitalization
» Illness or hospitalization for a family member
» Death of a loved one
» Changes at home and/or family
» Challenges with home or school
» Change/loss of job
» Losing something that they value
» New relationship
» Marriage
» Pregnancy or giving birth
» A breakup or divorce
» Neglect, abuse, or assault

This list is quasidefinitive though, as scientists don't actually have a certain cause of OCD.
What we do know, however, is how to help someone with ocd. Someone with OCD can:
» Go to OCD clinics
» Join a support group
» Get help from local affiliates
» Get help from a global partner
» If it gets to the suicide point, dial 988
